I do a weekly review every Friday afternoon. It takes about 20 minutes. The goal is simple: close the week cleanly, capture loose ends, and know exactly what needs attention next week.

## Why It Works

Without a review, tasks accumulate in a vague mental backlog that creates low-level anxiety. The review externalises everything — what's done, what's not, what matters next. After doing it, I feel genuinely lighter.

## The Process

### 1. Clear inboxes (5 min)

Go through email, messages, and any physical notes. Process each one:
- If it's actionable → capture it in my task system
- If it's reference → file it
- If it's noise → delete it

Don't act on anything right now. Just process.

### 2. Review the week (5 min)

Scan through what I worked on:
- What did I complete?
- What didn't get done? Why?
- Any commitments I made but haven't logged?

I look at my `.tasks/done/` folder and my calendar. Two minutes.

### 3. Update active tasks (5 min)

Look at what's in `.tasks/active/`:
- Is each task still relevant?
- Is anything blocked?
- Is the priority still right?

Move anything that's become irrelevant back to backlog, or delete it entirely.

### 4. Plan the next week (5 min)

Pick 1–3 things that must happen next week. Write them down. These are the non-negotiables.

Everything else is a "would be nice."

## What I Don't Do

- I don't review every project in depth
- I don't write a long reflection
- I don't plan every day of the next week

The review is a maintenance ritual, not a planning session. Planning happens separately.

## Friday Afternoon Is Key

Doing this on Friday means I'm not anxious over the weekend, and Monday morning starts with clarity instead of a scramble. The half-hour investment pays back in full.

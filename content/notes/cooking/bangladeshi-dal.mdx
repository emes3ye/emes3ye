This is the dal I make when I need something fast, filling, and that tastes like home. Red lentils (masoor) cook quickly and break down into a smooth, comforting bowl.

## Ingredients

*Serves 2–3*

- 200g red lentils (masoor dal), rinsed
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 medium tomatoes, chopped (or half a tin)
- 1 green chilli, slit (optional)
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- ½ tsp chilli powder (adjust to taste)
- Salt to taste
- 2 tbsp neutral oil or ghee
- Fresh coriander to serve

## Method

**1. Cook the lentils**

Add lentils to a pot with 600ml water and ½ tsp turmeric. Bring to a boil, skim any foam, then simmer for 15–20 minutes until fully soft. Add more water if it thickens too much.

**2. Tarka (tempering)**

Heat oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds — they should sizzle immediately. Add onion and cook until golden (7–8 minutes). Add garlic, ginger, and green chilli; cook 2 minutes. Add tomatoes, remaining turmeric, coriander powder, and chilli powder. Cook until the tomato breaks down and oil separates (5–6 minutes).

**3. Combine**

Pour the tarka into the lentils. Stir, taste, adjust salt. Simmer together for 5 minutes. If too thick, add a splash of hot water.

**4. Finish**

Remove from heat. Add a squeeze of lemon if you like. Garnish with fresh coriander.

## Serving

Serve with plain basmati rice or roti. A dollop of ghee on top is traditional and genuinely makes it better.

## Notes

- Lentils foam when boiling — skim it off for a cleaner result.
- The tarka is where the flavour lives. Don't rush it.
- Leftovers thicken overnight — add water when reheating.
- For a smoother dal, blend a portion before adding the tarka.
